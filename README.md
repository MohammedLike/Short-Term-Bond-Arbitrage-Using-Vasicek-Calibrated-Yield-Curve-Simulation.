## 📖 Overview
This repository contains a quantitative finance project that implements a **Short-Term Bond Arbitrage** strategy. The core of the strategy relies on simulating and forecasting interest rate term structures using a calibrated **Vasicek Interest Rate Model**. By comparing the theoretical yield curve generated by the model against empirical market rates, the algorithm identifies transient mispricings in short-term bonds to execute profitable, mean-reverting arbitrage trades.

## 🧠 The Vasicek Model
The Vasicek model is a mathematical model describing the evolution of interest rates. It is a type of one-factor short-rate model that describes interest rate movements as driven by only one source of market risk. The model is defined by the stochastic differential equation:

$$dr_t = a(b - r_t)dt + \sigma dW_t$$

Where:
* **$r_t$**: The short-term interest rate at time $t$.
* **$a$**: The speed of mean reversion.
* **$b$**: The long-term mean level.
* **$\sigma$**: The volatility of the interest rate.
* **$W_t$**: A Wiener process (Standard Brownian Motion).

This project calibrates these parameters ($a, b, \sigma$) using historical yield data (e.g., via Maximum Likelihood Estimation or Ordinary Least Squares) to simulate realistic future yield curves.

## 🚀 Arbitrage Strategy
1. **Calibration**: Fetch historical short-term treasury yields and calibrate the Vasicek parameters.
2. **Simulation**: Generate multiple paths of future short rates using Monte Carlo simulations.
3. **Pricing**: Construct the theoretical zero-coupon bond prices and the corresponding theoretical yield curve.
4. **Signal Generation**: Calculate the spread between the empirical market yield curve and the theoretical curve.
    * *Overpriced Bonds (Yield too low)*: Sell short.
    * *Underpriced Bonds (Yield too high)*: Buy long.
5. **Execution**: Hold positions until the market curve reverts to the theoretical mean (convergence).
